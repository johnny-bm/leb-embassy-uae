Lebanon & the UAE: A Strong Economic Partnership

Lebanon and the United Arab Emirates share deep economic and social ties, rooted in decades of partnership and a large, active Lebanese community in the UAE.

The Embassy of Lebanon in Abu Dhabi actively supports Lebanese businesses, entrepreneurs, and investors operating in the United Arab Emirates — providing document services, commercial attestations, and guidance on bilateral trade channels.

Lebanese entrepreneurs represent one of the most active business communities in the UAE, contributing across sectors including trade, finance, hospitality, real estate, engineering, and the creative industries.

Lebanon–UAE Relations

Diplomatic relations between Lebanon and the UAE were established in 1971. Since then, the bilateral relationship has grown to encompass trade, investment, cultural exchange, and strong people-to-people ties.

Lebanese Community in the UAE

The Lebanese community is one of the most established expatriate communities in the United Arab Emirates, with a strong presence across Abu Dhabi, Dubai, and the Northern Emirates.

Bilateral Trade Overview

The UAE is one of Lebanon's leading trade partners in the Arab world. Lebanese exports to the UAE include food products, beverages, jewellery, chemicals, and industrial goods. UAE exports to Lebanon include petroleum products, aluminium, and manufactured goods.

Lebanese Exports to UAE

  • Food and agricultural products
  • Beverages and tobacco
  • Gold jewellery and precious metals
  • Chemicals and pharmaceuticals
  • Machinery and electrical equipment
  • Paper and printing products

UAE Exports to Lebanon

  • Petroleum products and mineral fuels
  • Aluminium and aluminium products
  • Plastics and chemical products
  • Machinery and transport equipment
  • Iron and steel products
  • Manufactured consumer goods

Key Sectors

  • Banking and financial services
  • Hospitality and F&B
  • Real estate and construction
  • Healthcare and pharmaceuticals
  • Technology and professional services
  • Education and training

Embassy Services for Businesses

The Embassy provides a range of document services essential for Lebanese businesses and investors operating in the United Arab Emirates.

Commercial Document Authentication

The Embassy authenticates and attests a wide range of commercial documents for use between Lebanon and the UAE, including:

  • Company establishment contracts
  • Certificates of origin (Lebanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation)
  • Commercial invoices (4‰ of invoice value, min 166 AED)
  • Business powers of attorney
  • Employment contracts
  • Business acts and declarations

View Fee Schedule

Powers of Attorney for Business

Lebanese nationals and companies operating in the UAE may organise the following powers of attorney at the Embassy:

  • General POA — managing real estate (368 AED)
  • General POA — banking purposes (368 AED)
  • General POA — establishing companies (185 AED)
  • Special POA — sale or purchase of specific property (184 AED)
  • Pledge for registering cars in Lebanon (460 AED)
  • Customs pledge (460 AED)

Full POA Fee Table

Non-Objection & Pledge Services

The Embassy issues declarations and sponsorship pledges required for business or legal processes:

  • Non-Objection — Dual Nationality (251 AED)
  • Pledge of Sponsorship in UAE (460 AED) — applicant must attend in person
  • Declaration / Sponsorship / Pledge (460 AED)
  • Concessions / Tanazol (280 AED)
  • Revocation of Power of Attorney (276 AED)

Investing Between Lebanon & the UAE

Whether you are a Lebanese entrepreneur expanding into the UAE market, or a UAE-based investor exploring opportunities in Lebanon, the following information provides a starting point.

Setting Up in the UAE

Lebanese nationals looking to establish businesses in the UAE should refer to the relevant UAE authorities. The Embassy can assist with document authentication required as part of company formation processes.

  • Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED): www.added.gov.ae
  • Dubai Economy & Tourism (DET): www.det.gov.ae
  • UAE Ministry of Economy: www.economy.gov.ae

Investing in Lebanon

For information on investment opportunities and the business environment in Lebanon, the following institutions provide guidance and support:

  • Investment Development Authority of Lebanon (IDAL): www.idal.com.lb
  • Banque du Liban (Central Bank): www.bdl.gov.lb
  • Chamber of Commerce, Industry & Agriculture of Beirut: www.ccib.org.lb

Embassy Role

The Embassy of Lebanon in Abu Dhabi does not provide investment advice or legal counsel. For business and legal matters, please consult a licensed professional or the appropriate UAE or Lebanese authority.
